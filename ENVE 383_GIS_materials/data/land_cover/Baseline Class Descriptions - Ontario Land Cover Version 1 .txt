ONTARIO LAND COVER BASELINE CLASS DESCRIPTIONS

VALUES
11. Prairie
Ground layer dominated by prairie graminoids. Tree and shrub cover < 25%.

12. Savannah
Ground layer dominated by prairie graminoids. Variable cover of open-grown trees. Tree cover: 25 to 35%.

13. Alvar
Level, unfractured limestone bedrock or patchy mosaic of bare and rock pavement and shallow substrates. Vegetative cover < 25%.

14. Dune
Exposed sands formed by extant or historical shoreline or aeolian processes. Vegetative cover < 25%.

15. Meadow
Open herbaceous or graminoid communities. Tree and shrub cover < 25%.

16. Shrubland
Shrub community. Tree cover < 25%. Shrub cover => 25%.

17. Barren
Primarily open areas. Includes: exposed non-calcareous bedrock, mineral barren, shorelines, cliffs or bluffs. Tree cover < 10%. Shrub cover < 25%.

18. Sparse treed
Primarily open areas with sparse tree cover. Includes: exposed non-calcareous bedrock, mineral barren, shorelines, cliffs or bluffs. Tree cover: 10 to 25%.

21. Coniferous forest
Tree cover => 25%. Upland coniferous tree species => 75% canopy cover > 2m in height.

22. Mixedwood forest
Tree cover => 25%. Upland coniferous tree species => 25% and upland deciduous tree species => 25% of canopy cover > 2m in height.

23. Deciduous forest
Tree cover => 25%. Upland deciduous tree species => 75% canopy cover > 2m in height.

24. Transitional forest
Natural and/or anthropogenic disturbance in areas previously coniferous, mixedwood or deciduous forest. Includes new tree plantings. Tree cover and height in various stages of recovery.

25. Hedge row
Trees with linear organization and uniform tree height. Tree cover => 60%. Trees > 2m in height.

31. Coniferous treed swamp
Water table seasonally or permanently at, near or above substrate surface. Tree cover => 25%. Hydrophytic coniferous tree species => 75% of canopy cover.

32. Mixedwood treed swamp
Water table seasonally or permanently at, near or above substrate surface. Tree cover => 25%. Hydrophytic coniferous tree species => 25% and hydrophytic deciduous tree species => 25% of canopy cover.

33. Deciduous treed swamp
Water table seasonally or permanently at, near or above substrate surface. Tree cover => 25%. Hydrophytic deciduous tree species => 75% of canopy cover.

34. Transitional treed swamp
Water table seasonally or permanently at, near or above substrate surface. Natural and/or anthropogenic disturbance in areas previously coniferous, mixedwood or deciduous treed swamp. Includes new tree plantings. Tree cover and height in various stages of recovery. 

35. Thicket swamp
Water table seasonally or permanently at, near or above substrate surface. Tree cover < 25%. Hydrophytic shrubs => 25%.

36. Bog
Water table seasonally or permanently at, near or above substrate surface. Ombrotrophic peatland. Open, shrub or treed communities. Tree cover < 25%. Low ericaceous (<2m) shrubs dominate. Sphagnum peat substrate.

37. Fen
Water table seasonally or permanently at, near or above substrate surface. Mineotrophic peatland. Open, shrub or treed communities. Tree cover < 25%. Sedges, grasses and low (<2m) ericaceous shrubs dominate. Brown moss substrate.

38. Marsh
Water table seasonally or permanently at, near or above substrate surface. Dominated by graminoids or hydrophytic macrophytes. Tree and shrub cover < 25%.

41. Water
Water depth > 2 m. Absence of hydrophytic macrophytes, shrub or treed vegetation.

51. Built up area-pervious
Green space contained by anthropogenic development and/or maintained by human alteration. Includes: urban recreation areas (parks, golf courses, playing fields or ski runs), manicured lawns, urban brownfields or transportion right-of-ways.

52. Anthropogenic
Anthropogenic land use with impervious surface. Includes: residential, industrial, commercial, civic areas, mines, or aggregate extraction.

53. Cropland
Agricultural fields managed as row crops, orchards or vineyards.

54. Hay/pasture
Agricultural fields managed as perennial crops - hay or improved pasture.

55. Transportation
Highways, roads or airport runways.

99. Unclassified
Areas with insufficient data for classification.
